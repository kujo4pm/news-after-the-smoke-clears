The German Example - Zollverein

The Ruhr area in North Western area is Germany's coal mining heartland. Between 1997 to 2015 a partnership between government, industry and the unions oversaw the reduction of 72,820 jobs. Their credo and philosophy was "that no miner would be unemployed"

"But in the long run you have to look for an alternative for your country because the whole world is changing..." - Michael Mersmann, Director of Globalisation and European Policy, Union for Mining, Chemicals and Energy Workers
